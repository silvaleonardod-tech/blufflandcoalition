Our Work

Land Protection

Permanently shielding the blufflands from development — securing extraordinary landscapes for people, wildlife, and the communities that depend on them.

Why It Matters

A Landscape Worth Protecting Forever

The bluffs overlooking the Mississippi River in the La Crosse–La Crescent region are among the most ecologically significant landscapes in the Upper Midwest — rugged ridgelines, sandstone cliffs, and bluff prairies of continental ecological significance.

Without active protection, development pressure threatens to permanently alter the skyline, fragment wildlife habitat, and eliminate the open natural lands that define this region's character and quality of life.

The Bluffland Coalition coordinates land protection efforts across municipal, county, and nonprofit lines — ensuring that bluffland properties can be acquired and secured permanently for future generations.

A Century of Conservation

A Proud History

La Crosse's conservation tradition traces back to 1909. Led by Ellen Hixon, the community rallied to save Grandad Bluff from industrial exploitation — establishing a legacy of citizen-driven conservation that continues today.

1909
Community saves Grandad Bluff

Ellen Hixon leads campaign protecting the iconic bluff from exploitation.

1912
Hixon Forest established

Land donated to the city — permanently protected for public use.

1996
Mississippi Valley Conservancy formed

Protects nearly 800 acres in first three years of operation.

2002
La Crosse Bluffland Protection Program

City of La Crosse partners with MVC for eight-mile bluffland corridor. Recognized as Policymaker of the Year by Gathering Waters in 2006.

2016
Blufflands Plan adopted

Regional plan sets framework for coalition-wide conservation and recreation efforts.

Our Approach

How We Protect Land

The coalition uses multiple strategies to bring bluffland properties into permanent protection.

📜
Fee Simple Acquisition
Purchasing bluffland properties outright for permanent protection and public access.
🤝
Conservation Easements
Voluntary legal agreements permanently restricting development while keeping land in private hands.
💰
Grant Funding
Leveraging Wisconsin's Knowles-Nelson Stewardship Program, county grants, and private foundations.
🎁
Land Donations
Generous landowners have donated parcels serving as crucial links between protected areas.
