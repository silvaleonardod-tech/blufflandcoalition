Trails & Outdoors

Recreation in the Blufflands

A growing network of trails, overlooks, and natural areas connecting communities throughout the La Crosse–La Crescent region — for every skill level, every season.

Signature Route

The Bluffland Traverse

A point-to-point route running from the northern end of La Crosse County to the southern end, following the Mississippi River and the bluffs that overlook it.

The route connects communities using a combination of single-track, shared-use trail, gravel bike path, and road on public property. ORA Trails is actively working to acquire easements bringing more of the route into the woods and away from traffic.

Trail Network

Trails for Every Adventurer

The blufflands offer remarkable variety — from easy nature walks to challenging mountain bike climbs — across the La Crosse–La Crescent area.

Hixon Forest
La Crosse · Multiple access points

One of the most popular urban natural areas in the region, with miles of marked trails through oak woodland and bluffside terrain. Trails graded green/blue/black by difficulty.

🥾 Hiking🚵 MTB🎿 XC SkiAll levels
Quarry Trail (Cty Rd FA)
Town of Medary · 453 acres

Reconstructed prairie and beautiful driftless landscapes at the Mathy Quarry property. Picnic tables at the weather station trailhead make it great for families.

🥾 Hiking🏃 Trail Run❄️ Winter
Miller Bluff
Rim of the City Rd · La Crosse

Spectacular bluff-top trail with sandstone promontories offering sweeping views of the city and river. Excellent for birding — watch for bald eagles and hawks on thermals.

🥾 Hiking🦅 Birding📸 ViewsModerate
Crown Boulevard Trailhead
South La Crosse

A moderate hike through remote bluffland to overlooks and stunning rock formations. Home to rare native plants including amethyst shooting star, bloodroot, and pasqueflower.

🥾 Hiking🌸 WildflowersModerate
County Rd B Trailhead
Onalaska · W5845 Cty Rd B

Located just north of Onalaska off Hwy 16. An out-and-back hike climbing the bluffs with parking at the roadside kiosk. Beginner-friendly with marked trail boundaries.

🥾 HikingBeginner
Halfway Creek Trail
Holmen · Northern corridor

Part of the northern reach of the Bluffland Traverse, connecting Holmen toward Onalaska along the base of the bluffs. A key link in the growing regional network.

Year-Round Adventure

🌸
Spring
Wildflowers burst across bluff prairies. Migratory birds return in force. Ideal for guided hikes and birding walks on the ridgelines.
☀️
Summer
Full canopy shade on the trails. Peak wildflower season continues into late summer with volunteer restoration days and family outings.
🍂
Autumn
Stunning fall color along bluffs and the Mississippi valley. Prime hawk-watching season from overlooks. Crisp conditions for hiking and biking.
❄️
Winter
Many trails open for snowshoeing and cross-country skiing. The snow-covered bluffs offer a quiet, dramatic beauty unique to the Upper Mississippi Valley.

Trail Use Notes: Trails are marked with green (easy), blue (intermediate), and black (advanced) markers. For maps and current conditions, contact the City of La Crosse Parks & Recreation Department at 608-789-7533 or visit City Trail Maps.
