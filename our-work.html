Coalition News January 2026

La Crosse County Board Approves Four Bluffland Grants

The Bluffland Coalition announced the La Crosse County Board's approval of four separate bluffland grants — a major step forward for regional trail connectivity and habitat restoration. Leonardo Silva, Supervisor of the Town of Medary and a coalition member, highlighted the accomplishment as evidence of the coalition's growing impact after nearly a decade of sustained work.

The coalition's volunteer network continues to grow — hundreds of community members spend Saturday afternoons building trails and clearing invasive species from bluffland properties across the region.

Source: WIZM News, January 6, 2026

Coalition Grant Expands Community Trail Farm by 10 Acres

A $50,000 joint grant from the Bluffland Coalition and La Crosse County enabled ORA Trails to acquire 10 additional acres for the Community Trail Farm. The new land sits at the top of the bluff near Skyline Boulevard and Hagen Road, opening access from State Road Elementary, Chad Erickson Park, and surrounding neighborhoods.

Source: La Crosse Tribune, August 2025

Land ProtectionAugust 2025

80-Acre French Road Property Donated to City of Onalaska

Mississippi Valley Conservancy donated 80 acres of bluffland to the City of Onalaska, bringing the city's total conserved bluffland to 162 acres. The French Road property features dry cliffs, globally vulnerable prairie remnants, and rare oak woodlands above the Mississippi River valley — connected to existing Greenway parklands with a permanent conservation easement.

Source: La Crosse Tribune, August 2025

Community Trail Farm Launches — 270+ Acres, 15 Miles of Trails Planned

ORA Trails publicly launched the Community Trail Farm project — a 270+ acre site along Pammel Creek in the heart of the blufflands. Secured with a $1.15 million Knowles-Nelson Stewardship Program grant, the project will deliver 15 miles of world-class trails, a nature education center, and year-round access for all ages and abilities.

Land ProtectionFebruary 2023

County Board Votes on $150,000 Toward 277-Acre ORA Acquisition in Town of Shelby

The La Crosse County Board considered $150,000 from the Bluffland Protection Fund toward ORA Trails' acquisition of 277 acres of bluffland in the Town of Shelby — a DNR Knowles-Nelson Stewardship grant project that would extend trail connectivity to the southern corridor near Goose Island. Since 2017, roughly $87,000 had been drawn from the county's Bluffland Protection Fund before this vote.

Source: WIZM News, February 2023
TraverseNovember 5, 2022

Dr. Michael Borst Completes the Full 53-Mile Traverse

ORA Trails president Dr. Michael Borst — the creator of the Bluffland Traverse route — became the first to document a complete run of all 53 miles on November 5, 2022, from Camp Decorah in Holmen to Goose Island County Park. The accomplishment demonstrated the route's viability and galvanized community support for completing the trail.

Source: ORA Trails, November 2022

Land ProtectionDecember 2021

Kaplan Tract Adds 44 Acres to 1,200-Acre Corridor in Town of Medary

Mississippi Valley Conservancy purchased the 44-acre Kaplan property — featuring 150-year-old bur and white oaks, ancient prairie remnants, and sandstone bedrock fissures sheltering area wildlife. It adds to a connected corridor of more than 1,200 acres identified as an area of "continental ecological significance." Funded by Wisconsin's Knowles-Nelson Stewardship Program and the Paul E. Stry Foundation.

Source: Mississippi Valley Conservancy, December 2021

Coalition News2018 – 2019

Eight Organizations Sign Memorandum of Understanding — Coalition Officially Formed

La Crosse County became the first to sign the coalition's MOU in 2018; within months, seven more governments and nonprofits joined. Member municipalities commit 40 cents per capita annually for coalition coordination, plus $1 per capita over 25 years for local bluffland projects. A part-time coalition coordinator was hired soon after with early municipal contributions.

Source: Wisconsin Public Radio, 2019
Land Protection2002 – 2016

La Crosse Bluffland Protection Program — A Decade of Urban Conservation

From 2002 to 2009, the City of La Crosse and Mississippi Valley Conservancy jointly protected an eight-mile stretch of Mississippi River bluffs, leveraging city funds with Knowles-Nelson Stewardship grants. Nearly 1,000 acres were protected, including the 453-acre Mathy Quarry (the largest single tract). The City was recognized as "Policymaker of the Year" by Gathering Waters in 2006.

Source: Mississippi Valley Conservancy

Coalition News2016

The 2016 Blufflands Plan — A Regional Framework for Conservation & Recreation

The La Crosse Area Planning Committee published the Blufflands Plan in 2016 — establishing goals to permanently protect 6,000 acres of bluffland and build a multi-use trail from Goose Island to northern La Crosse County. A public survey found over 70% of area residents would support additional property taxes for bluffland protection.

Source: La Crosse Area Planning Committee, 2016; WPR
